About the CWRA

Residents working together since 2025

The Cocoa Works Residents' Association grew out of a campaign to fix serious failures in bike shed security. Residents from Mint, Cream, and Almond came together, held the developer to account, and secured a £15,440 investment in security upgrades. That experience showed what organised residents can achieve — and why a permanent, structured group is needed.

What we are

  • ✓An informal, self-elected group representing residents' interests
  • ✓A conduit between residents and Urban Bubble / Clarion/Latimer
  • ✓A group that raises communal issues and holds management to account

Who We Are

The people behind the CWRA

The Steering Group is made up of residents from Mint, Cream, and Almond — volunteers giving their time to make The Cocoa Works a better place for everyone.

Our Story

In August 2025, a series of bike thefts exposed serious weaknesses in the bike shed security — and a pattern of slow, inadequate responses from Urban Bubble and Clarion/Latimer. A small group of residents decided to act. Over several months, they organised formally, brought in police crime prevention experts, engaged security contractors, and kept up sustained pressure. The result: Clarion agreed a £15,440 goodwill contribution toward a full security upgrade.

That experience proved what organised residents can achieve. It also showed the need for a permanent, structured body — one that could tackle not just security, but any issue affecting residents' quality of life at The Cocoa Works. The CWRA Steering Group is the result.

Get Involved

Join the Residents' Association

Membership is free and open to all residents. The more members we have, the stronger our voice.

Why membership numbers matter: To be formally recognised under the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985, we need at least 50% of leaseholders to be full members. Every membership counts. Membership is currently free.
Who can join?

Full members (with voting rights)

Leaseholders of apartments at The Cocoa Works, including shared ownership leaseholders. One vote per apartment.

Associate members (no voting rights)

Renting tenants; adults over 18 living permanently with a leaseholder; employees of Latimer/Clarion or Urban Bubble who live here. You can attend and speak at meetings.
